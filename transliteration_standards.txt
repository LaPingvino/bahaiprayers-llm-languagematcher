BAHÁ'Í TRANSLITERATION STANDARDS (Shoghi Effendi System)

PURPOSE: Uniform spelling of Arabic/Persian terms in Roman alphabet. Key to original spelling, not pronunciation guide.

CORE PRINCIPLE: Arabic pronunciation in Arabic context, Persian in Persian context.

CRITICAL EXAMPLES:
بهاء الله = Bahá'u'lláh (NOT Bahaullah)
عبد البهاء = 'Abdu'l-Bahá (NOT Abdul-Baha)
اقدس = Aqdas (NOT Akdas)
رضوان = Riḍván (NOT Ridvan)
الله ابهی = Alláh-u-Abhá (NOT Allah-o-Abha)

COMMON ERRORS TO AVOID:
- Missing diacritics (á í ú ḥ ṣ ṭ ẓ ḍ)
- Wrong apostrophes (use ' not ')
- Vowel length errors (a/á, i/í, u/ú)
- Persian/Arabic mixing (th→s, dh→z in Persian)

EXCEPTIONS: Islam, Shah, Sultan = no diacritics when standalone; transliterated when in phrases (e.g., Muḥammad Sháh).
